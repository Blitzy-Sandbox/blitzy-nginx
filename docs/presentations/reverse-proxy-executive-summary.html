Section 11 · Recommended actions

Three prioritized actions for leadership

Ranked by impact on the two critical scorecard dimensions.

01 Critical Knowledge continuity
Formalize upstream state-machine knowledge transfer
Produce an ADR for ngx_http_upstream.c (509 commits, 7,247 LOC) covering the state-machine lifecycle, error recovery, and buffering modes. With both Sysoev and Dounin inactive, the 2 remaining contributors lack documented design rationale.
Evidence: 8 unresolved TODOs in upstream.c; no in-tree architecture docs.
02 High Quality investment
Harden ngx_event_pipe.c buffer management
48% bug-fix ratio on the data-transfer pump that powers every buffered upstream response. Two buffer-lifecycle TODOs at lines 590 and 721 have been unresolved since 2004.
Evidence: 45 of 92 commits are bug fixes; 30 logging call sites signal anticipated debugging.
03 Medium Architectural debt
Evaluate HTTP/Stream upstream consolidation
Parallel src/http/ngx_http_upstream*.c and src/stream/ngx_stream_upstream*.c double the maintenance surface with 6 duplicated balancer modules. Every new balancer feature must currently be implemented twice.
Evidence: c799c82fa "Stream: port from NGINX+" (2015); ~40% duplicate surface area.
What this tells us about execution Actions 1 and 2 address the two highest-leverage findings: they convert personal knowledge into institutional artifacts before any further contributor transition. Action 3 is optional consolidation; the first two are prerequisites for durable execution.
